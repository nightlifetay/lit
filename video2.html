Direct object could be a noun, noun phrase, or pronoun that identifies what or who receives the action of a transitive verb in a clause or sentence. Indirect Object Grammar is a means of expressing the content of statements, questions or other utterances, without quoting them explicitly as is done in direct speech. Using Indirect and Direct Grammar could be very tricky. In conversation it is all about how you are saying the object that determines if you are using indirect or direct grammar.

    Direct Object
      Examples: (Present Simple)
    • She said, “It’s cold.”
    • She said, “I'll run tomorrow.”
    • She said, “I can jump rope.”
    Indirect Object
      Examples: (Past Simple)
    • She said It was cold.
    • She said she will run tomorrow.
    • She said she could jump rope.
